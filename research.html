Research Interests

Photonics & Optical Systems

I am interested in the design and experimental realization of optical systems for imaging, spectroscopy, and sensing. My work focuses on building and characterizing custom optical setups, combining interferometry, holography, and ultrafast techniques to extract quantitative information from light–matter interactions.

Optical Design Interferometry vImaging Systems Ultrafast Optics Chatzakis Lab Page (link)

Nanophotonics & Micro/Nanoscale Devices

My research interests include engineering light–matter interactions at the micro- and nanoscale using dielectric, plasmonic, and metasurface-based structures. I am particularly motivated by device-level approaches that enable compact, scalable, and high-performance photonic components for sensing and information processing.

Metasurfaces Plasmonics Nanodevices Integrated Photonics Electro-Optic/Magneto-Optic Modulators

Quantum Photonics & Sensing

I am interested in quantum-enabled photonic systems that leverage defects, cavities, and engineered materials to achieve enhanced sensitivity and functionality. My goal is to develop integrated quantum photonic platforms for applications in sensing, communication, and hybrid quantum networks.
