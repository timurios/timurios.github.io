Current Research

Quantum Photonic Devices

Developing integrated quantum photonic systems for on-chip light–matter interactions. Exploring nanostructured materials and nonlinear effects for efficient photon routing, detection, and manipulation.

Nanomagnetism and Spintronics

Simulating magnetization dynamics in FeCo nanowires using OOMMF to study spin-transfer torque and anisotropy effects. Investigating scalable architectures for magnetic memory and logic applications.

Optoelectronic Sensing for Intelligent Robotics

Built FPGA-controlled color and distance sensing systems for autonomous rover platforms. Integrated TCS3200 color sensors and IR communication modules to enable environmental feedback and inter-robot communication.

Past Work

High-Energy Detector Instrumentation (CMS, CERN)

Characterized silicon sensors for the CMS detector upgrade at CERN. Collaborated with international teams to support high-luminosity LHC operations, contributing to the world’s largest particle physics experiment.
