About Me

I am an undergraduate researcher with a background in Applied Physics and Electrical Engineering, currently applying to PhD programs. My academic interests lie in photonics, micro- and nanoscale devices, and quantum-enabled sensing and imaging systems. I am particularly motivated by research that bridges fundamental physical principles with experimentally realizable and scalable technologies.

My research experience spans optical imaging, spectroscopy, detector systems, and computational data analysis. I have worked on the design and implementation of quantitative phase imaging and digital holographic microscopy systems, where I combined optical system design with algorithm development to improve reconstruction quality and processing efficiency. I have also conducted research in Raman spectroscopy, developing computational methods to remove experimental artifacts and improve signal-to-noise ratios in custom-built spectroscopic setups. In parallel, I have experience in high-energy and materials-related research, including characterization of silicon photomultipliers for particle detectors and modeling of dielectric metasurfaces for beam shaping applications. More recently, my work has focused on ultrafast optical spectroscopy of quantum materials, investigating defect-related photoluminescence in hexagonal boron nitride for applications in quantum photonics and sensing. I enjoy working at the intersection of experiment, computation, and device physics, and I am especially drawn to interdisciplinary environments that connect physics, electrical engineering, and materials science. Through my research experiences, conference presentations, and collaborations across institutions, I have become committed to pursuing a research career focused on developing next-generation photonic and quantum technologies. I am seeking doctoral training that will allow me to deepen my expertise in nanofabrication, quantum optics, and device integration, while contributing to collaborative, full-cycle research from concept to experimental realization.
